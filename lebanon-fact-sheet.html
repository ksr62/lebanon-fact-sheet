Introduction

In 1920, the French demarcated the region of Lebanon. On November 22nd, the region officially achieved independence becoming the Lebanese Republic. However, from 1975 to 1990, the country underwent a civil war followed by year of social, political, and economic instability.

Lebanon is located in the Middle East, bordering the Mediterranean Sea between Israel and Syria. Geography

Location

Being located near the Mediterranean, Lebanon experiences mild to cool, wet winters with hot, dry summers. However, Lebanon also has mountain ranges that are known for heavy, winter snowstorms. Lebanon is also known for natural hazards such as earthquakes, sandstorms, and dust storms.
